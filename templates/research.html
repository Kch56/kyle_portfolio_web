Research Summary: Esri GeoAI for the Charlotte Risk Intelligence Dashboard

This research explores how Esri’s GeoAI capabilities can strengthen a citywide risk intelligence dashboard by combining GIS layers with machine learning to support predictive risk modeling, real-time monitoring, and decision-ready outputs.

Why GeoAI matters

  • Risk is spatial: exposure, access, density, hazards, and response constraints all vary by location.
  • GeoAI helps detect patterns and forecast risk areas instead of only mapping what already happened.
  • Outputs can be delivered as map layers that planners and operations can interpret quickly.

Key capabilities (high level)

  • GeoAnalytics + ML: identify hotspots and forecast risk using large spatial datasets.
  • Deep learning (imagery): detect features that support risk context (built environment signals).
  • Notebooks: Python workflows to preprocess data, prototype models, and publish outputs.
  • Real-time analysis: ingest live feeds (weather/sensors) to update risk layers.

How it applies to Charlotte

  • Risk prediction: anticipate higher-risk areas based on historical + environmental signals.
  • Dynamic zones: update zones based on development, density changes, or incident trends.
  • Scenario simulation: test how risk changes under conditions (heat, storms, special events).
  • Decision support: provide map-ready outputs for planners, leadership, and field awareness.

Implementation considerations

  • Data access + quality is the biggest dependency.
  • Model outputs must be validated before influencing operations.
  • Keep the system explainable and auditable (transparent inputs, documented methods).

Research Summary: Fire Department Technology

This research explains why technology should be treated as operational infrastructure. The focus is on how GIS intelligence, analytics, automation, and standardized workflows improve planning quality and reduce repetitive work—especially in growing cities.

Main themes

  • GIS is essential because risk, access, and coverage are spatial problems.
  • Automation reduces manual overhead and improves consistency.
  • Dashboards and decision tools translate data into action.
  • Grant pathways make adoption feasible through phased investment.
